General info
What is this app about?
The primary purpose of this app is to help people plan, manage and collaborate on gatherings easier and more efficiently.
Can I use this app for other events like baby showers, birthdays and other events?
While this app was originally intended for potlucks, you can use this app for events as you see fit.
What are some of the key features about this app?
  • Create your own custom lists (e.g. Appetizers, Board games, Snacks, Movies, etc).
  • Easily RSVP with as little or many items you wish.
  • Easily track the total amount of guests attending, not coming and undecided.
  • An easy-to-use dashboard which provides the information you need fast
  • Easily notify groups of individuals or individual guests at a time.
  • Automated email reminders that remind guests to rsvp and that the event is coming.
  • Settings that allow you to turn notifications on/off and to customize when event reminders get sent out (Host only)
  • Maintain email distribution lists for common groups of people you invite
  • Easily promote guest to assist you (e.g. cohost) your events
Do I have to pay for this?
As this is currently in alpha, this is free. In return I would like to get your honest feedback about what you like, what you don't like, what could be improved and any potential future features that would encourage you to continue using it.
What should I do if I encounter a problem with the app or if there are defects?
Email me at cavenator@gmail.com and provide me a description of what the defect is and how to reproduce it (e.g. the steps that you took in order to encounter this defect).
Quick Overview
Options
  • My Groups:This is where you manage common groups of friends such as coworkers, college buddies, family, etc
  • Settings:When you are hosting events, these settings adjust notifications and configure reminders. Settings for when you are a guest to a event will be coming shortly!
As a host
  • Potluck statistics:This is a dashboard which indicates the amount of people coming, # of items they are bringing versus the amount of items requested
  • My list:This is where you manage your list. While lists may seem ambiguous, the intent is to keep it generic enough to include things like boardgames, chairs, silverware and many more (including potluck items)
  • Invite Guest:This is the automated way of inviting guests (especially if you want to add multiple guests at a time). As implied, you provide a comma-delimited email string which sends invites out and adds them to the guest list (when they sign up/login, they get synced up with events and their name fields populate automatically on the guest list)
  • The guest list: As this implies, you can manage your guests here. You do have the option of entering them in manually if they have no email but I would advise that you use Invite Guest if you want to add more than one at a time.
  • The other options filter the guest list by whos coming, whos not coming, etc
As a guest
  • Available items:Quick view of the items that the host is asking for.
  • Items others are bringing:Quick view of what others are bringing. Helpful in getting an idea of what others are bringing so you know how to fill in gaps.
Finally, the RSVP. This is pretty self explanatory except that when you declare you are coming, you can add items to your rsvp (with as little as none or as many as you can feasibly bring)
Getting Started
What do I need to do in order to start using this?
First, create an account.
What is the bare minimum that I need to provide in order to create an event?
After you create an account, you should be able to create events pretty easily. At a bare minimum, you just need to provide a name for the event, the start/end dates of the event, and when you need guests to rsvp by.
How do I invite guests?
As a host, you'll be able to invite guest using the Invite guest option when you are viewing the details of the event. You'll be able to invite guests via comma-delimited emails (e.g. email1, email2, email3). This is the preferred method of adding the guests to your event since the system will automatically take care of adding them rather than you manually providing that information via The guest list.
Can I manually add guests without using the Invite guest option?
I understand that certain guests may not have email and would rather call you instead of using the system. For this purpose, this app will allow you to rsvp on their behalf. You can do so by using the The guest list option (enter guest manually and rsvp for them).
Any other advice for getting started?
I would recommend creating your lists before inviting guests. While it is possible for your guests to rsvp without declaring what they are bringing, it might be good for them to get an idea of what is needed for the event.
List FAQ
What kind of lists can I create?
You can pretty much create any type of list you wish: Appetizers, Snacks, Beer/Alcohol, Boardgames, Movies, Xbox games, etc. For example, create a list with Games as your category and enter each game on a separate field (you can dynamically add as many items as you wish for a particular category).
Do you have any recommendations on effectively creating list?
You can pretty much create any type of list you wish: Appetizers, Snacks, Beer/Alcohol, Boardgames, Movies, Xbox games, etc. However, it's best that you be as specific as possible with your categories so that your items will be easily categorized and specific. A category of Food and having 30+ items is not an easy way to provide lists for your guests AND does not provide you an easy way to see what guests are bringing. Therefore, having specific categories like Snacks, you can then differentiate items like Chips/Salsa, Pretzels, Peanuts, Bite-size candy. The next list could then be Appetizers with items such as Spring Rolls, Nachos, Taquitos, Buffalo Wings.
What is this Desired number of items that I am seeing when I create my list?
That is meant to represent the number of items you need for guest to contribute for the potluck. For example, you create a list of category Boardgames with a host quantity of 2 and provide 5 games as suggestions. This is primarily for you to keep track of whether or not your "quota" for that particular category has been met.
My Groups FAQ
What is this "My Groups" thing?
My Groups is analogous to an email distribution list. This way, you can manage multiple email distributions and easily import groups into your event. This is helpful if you do plan multiple events with the same group of friends, coworkers, etc.
So how do I make a group?
Ensure that when you create a group, you supply a name and a list of comma-delimited emails (e.g. email1, email2, email3) and so on. That is it.
When I first create the list, there is a big textbox and when I want to edit it, the list is itemized. Why is that?
It's my understanding that when one first creates an email distribution group, one pretty much has a solid list of people in mind. Therefore, when modifications to the email distribution list are needed, it's usually adding/removing a couple of guests at a time. I found this much easier to manage lists that were already created rather than modifying in a large textbox.
So how do I add emails to an existing group?
Click the button Add more button. Each new box ONLY should contain one email at a time if you want it to work properly.
Cohost FAQ
What can a cohost do?
Since the event creator is the only one who can promote their guest to cohost, the cohost is a designated guest who can assist in managing the event details (such as inviting guests, managing lists for guests to choose from, etc). The only thing a cohost cannot do is promote other guests.
How can I promote a guest to cohost?
For a event you create, click on the The Guest List. You'll see a + next to the people you can promote. Once you click the +, they will be promoted. To demote them and make them an ordinary guest again, click the - instead.
Why don't I see +/- next to some entries?
You will only see +/- once your guest actually has an email address

Messaging FAQ
When I send someone a message, how does that work?
The message gets routed to their declared email account. Therefore, they will get the message as you send it.
Why can I message some people and others I cannot?
Thats because the host added them to the system without specifying what their email address is. You are only allowed to message guests that have email addresses declared.
i keep getting messages from letsgather.apartyapp@gmail.com. what's this about?
That is the automated emailer that sends messages to users and guests of this sytem. Until I switch over from using Gmail to a more scalable SMTP server like SendGrid, the name remains. therefore, please advise people to not label this as spam. When you send a message using this app, you'll see the subject where it belongs but the message will start with "a message from name" followed by the message contents.
RSVP FAQ (Guest)
I want to RSVP for an event I was invited to. How difficult is it?
It should be pretty easy. Once you sign in, select the event you wish to rsvp to. Click "Modify RSVP" to enter your response.
What is the bare minimum I need to RSVP?
By default, your response is Undecided. You only need to change that answer. However, once you decide you are going, you will be presented another list of options. Those options include:
  • # of guests you are bringing (excluding you)
  • Any items you wish to bring
You also have the option of providing a comment (or updating your comment to let the host know of any updates).
Do I have to RSVP with an item?
No. You may decide what to bring to the event later but inform the host you plan on coming.
What if I want to bring more than one item (or if I am answering on behalf of a guest I am bringing)?
If you are bringing someone to an event you are invited to (such as a friend and/or partner), you may add items to your rsvp and just provide a short comment saying that you and your guests are bringing those items. This app allows for multiple items to be umbrellaed under one RSVP response (e.g. You could be bringing a guest and each of you brings an item. Therefore, you can add another item to your rsvp (Just press the Add more button).
Settings FAQ
What kind of settings are available?
Right now, settings are only available for hosts (I got feedback from a particular host. Guest settings per event are coming soon). Those settings are as follows:
  • Notify me when a guest rsvps yes
  • Notify me when a guest rsvps no
  • Notify me of all guest response changes (use tthis with discretion since you might get spammed depending on the fickleness of your guest)
  • Send out rsvp reminders X days before the rsvp deadline
  • Send out Event reminders X days before the start date
What is this future events setting all about?
You can configure future settings that will apply to all future events you create (hence host). Therefore, if there are common settings you want applied to all events going forward, set it up appropriately. It still gives you the freedom to configure specific event settings if the need arises
When are guest settings coming?
Hopefully very soon! The only setting that I can think of for the time being is Notify me of any major event changes (applies to event times, description, location). There will be more for when you have cohost settings and even more depending on what type of feedback I get.
